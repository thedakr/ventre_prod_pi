### 1. Agent Selection for MVP
**Decision:** Proceed with 3-agent MVP
- Chief of Staff (orchestrator)
- Market Discovery (customer validation)
- Methodology Coach (framework teaching)

**Rationale:**
- Focus on highest-risk early-stage validation phase
- Chief of Staff provides guidance for beginners
- Market Discovery prevents #1 failure mode (building what nobody wants)
- Methodology Coach builds entrepreneurial thinking capacity
- Product/Tech, Finance, and Strategy agents deferred to later cohorts

### 3. Cohort Format
**Decision:** Virtual cohort

**Implementation:**
- Zoom sessions for facilitated group meetings
- Web portal for AI agent access
- Technology access required (AI-based program)
- PM may provide laptops/AI subscriptions for participants who need them

**Rationale:**
- Aligns with AI agent delivery model (requires internet)
- Accessibility for distributed participants
- Lower logistical overhead than in-person

### 4. Development Timeline
**Decision:** 4-week development timeline accepted

**Action Required:**
- PM to confirm feasibility with AI Engineer this week
- Validate engineer capacity for scope

**Risk Mitigation:**
- Single portal approach (MVP-SINGLE-PORTAL-APPROACH.md) significantly reduces dev scope
- Chief of Staff orchestration pattern reduces from 3-6 separate chat interfaces to 1
- Estimated 3-4 weeks (vs original 6-8 weeks) with simplified architecture

### 5. Facilitation Model
**Decision:** Co-facilitation by Education Specialist + PM

**Exploration:**
- Considering whether AI Chief of Staff can partially facilitate
- Testing in early cohorts to see if human facilitation can be reduced over time
- Long-term vision: AI-facilitated at scale with minimal human intervention

**Rationale:**
- Two facilitators provide coverage and complementary strengths
- PM brings product/strategic lens, Education Specialist brings curriculum/pedagogy
- Co-facilitation model aligns with PM taking on agent development (40% in Phases 3-4)

---

### 6. Funding Model
**Decision:** Free for all participants

**Implementation:**
- No participant fees
- PM may provide laptops or AI subscriptions if needed for access
- Grant funding pathway to pursue AFTER learning from initial cohorts

**Rationale:**
- Aligns with underserved population focus (â‰¤150% poverty line)
- Removes financial barrier to participation
- Sustainability planning deferred until post-pilot learnings validate model

**Note:** Budget implications to be determined (laptop costs, LLM API costs, facilitation time)

---

### 7. Post-Cohort Support
**Decision:** Ongoing access + cohort progression model

**Implementation:**
- Participants continue to access AI agents after cohort ends (duration TBD based on resources)
- Potential progression to further cohorts at different stages of business development
  - Example: Cohort 1 = Ideation/Validation, Cohort 2 = Building/Launching, Cohort 3 = Growth/Scale
- Long-term vision: Self-support at scale (AI agents enable ongoing support without facilitation)

**Rationale:**
- Entrepreneurship is a journey, not a 4-week event
- Ongoing access provides continued value and learning
- Staged cohorts allow participants to progress as their business evolves
- AI agents make ongoing support economically viable (vs human coaching)
